Title: Boot-Safe Strapping Pin Latch Circuit for ESP32-C6

Description:
This circuit allows GPIO8, GPIO9, and GPIO15 (strapping pins on the ESP32-C6) to be safely used as regular GPIOs after boot without interfering with boot mode configuration. It uses a latching relay triggered by EXIO7 (a user-defined control pin) to permanently connect the strapping pins to external logic after the ESP32 has successfully booted.

Components:
- 3PDT Latching Relay (mechanical or solid-state)
- EXIO7 (GPIO output from ESP32, set HIGH after boot)
- 3x Series resistors (1k) for GPIO15, GPIO8, GPIO9 (optional but recommended)
- 3x Pull-up resistors (10k) on strapping pins (to ensure HIGH at boot)
- 3x Protection diodes (Schottky 1N5712) on control line
- Flyback diode (1N4148 or equivalent) across relay coil
- Optional: Status LED + resistor for debug

Operation:
1. At power-on/reset:
   - Relay is unlatched; GPIO15/8/9 are disconnected from external devices (e.g., motor driver AIN pins)
   - Internal ESP32 pull-ups ensure strapping pins read HIGH
   - ESP32 boots into correct mode

2. After boot:
   - Firmware sets EXIO7 HIGH briefly
   - This HIGH triggers the latching relay (via diode and cap if needed)
   - Relay latches, permanently connecting:
     - GPIO15 → External logic
     - GPIO8  → AIN1
     - GPIO9  → AIN2

3. EXIO7 can return LOW; the relay remains latched until next power cycle

Design Notes:
- The 1N5712 Schottky diodes on the EXIO7-to-latch trigger line prevent backfeeding current when EXIO7 goes LOW
- Pull-up resistors ensure HIGH logic level is reliably sampled during boot, even with nearby capacitance or leakage paths
- Series resistors prevent ringing or overshoot into driver ICs
- Flyback diode is required to protect EXIO7 or transistor from relay coil inductive kick

Use Cases:
- Motor driver AIN1/AIN2 connections
- Mux/expander resets or address pins
- Any scenario requiring ESP32 strapping pin reuse post-boot
