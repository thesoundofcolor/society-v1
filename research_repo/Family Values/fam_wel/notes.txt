Notes for this influence:

Family Values -> Welfare Support = +0.09

Family values tend to correlate with support for family-targeted welfare policies, such as childcare assistance and parental support programs. At the same time, stronger family values often emphasize family-provided care over state intervention, which can reduce support for larger welfare systems. As a result, the net direct influence on welfare support is modest and mixed. Family values may slightly reinforce family-centric assistance, while welfare itself exerts a stronger, asymmetric influence that can weaken traditional family reliance rather than being driven by it.
