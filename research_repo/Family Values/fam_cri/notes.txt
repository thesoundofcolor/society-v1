Family Values - > Crime = -0.26

Strong family values exert a substantial negative influence on crime. Evidence consistently shows that stable family environments, positive parenting practices, emotional support, and effective supervision reduce the likelihood of criminal behavior by fostering self-control, moral development, and social attachment. While family structure alone does not determine outcomes, healthy family dynamics function as a major protective factor that mitigates risk, especially during early development and adolescence.
