Family Values -> Gun Rights = +0.04

When people build families, especially when children enter the picture, the relationship with firearms often changes. Guns become less abstract and more symbolic: a promise of protection, a tool of responsibility, a tradition handed down rather than merely owned. In many homes, particularly outside dense urban centers, firearms are woven into family identity.

This influence does not directly expand or contract legal gun rights. Instead, it swells the presence of guns within society. And in this model, gun population matters. Widespread ownership reinforces norms, expectations, and political gravity around firearms, quietly amplifying what “gun rights” means in practice, even when the laws themselves remain unchanged.
