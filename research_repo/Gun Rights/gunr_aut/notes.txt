Gun Rights -> Authoritarianism = 0.0

No direct pairwise influence is assigned. Current evidence does not support a linear causal relationship between civilian gun rights and the emergence of authoritarianism.

Higher-order note:
If an effect exists, it is NONLINEAR and conditional. Gun rights do not reliably prevent authoritarian drift, but may constrain the exercise of authoritarian power once repression escalates beyond a threshold (e.g., targeted violence, domination of specific groups, or collapse of democratic procedures). In this sense, gun rights function as a contextual dampener on authoritarian output rather than a driver of regime type. This effect is contingent, asymmetric, and unsuitable for linear pairwise modeling.
