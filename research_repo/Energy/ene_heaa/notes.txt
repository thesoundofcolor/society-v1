Energy Production -> Healthcare Access: +0.29  [01-02-2026 ~Evan L.]

The literature consistently shows that reliable energy production is a foundational requirement for healthcare access. Electrification enables core medical functions: lighting, refrigeration, sterilization, diagnostics, communications, and emergency services—and produces large, measurable increases in service availability, especially in rural and off-grid contexts. While energy alone is not sufficient to guarantee healthcare quality or utilization, it functions as a gatekeeping infrastructure without which access collapses. Given its first-order enabling role, a strong positive weight is warranted.


Reliable energy production is a necessary infrastructure layer for healthcare access.

The Energies review shows that electricity is required for:

Vaccine refrigeration
Sterilization
Lighting for emergency and nighttime care
Diagnostic equipment
Communications and data systems
-------------------------------------------------------------
The WHO/Health Policy & Planning implementation study in Ghana and Uganda found:

24-hour emergency services rose from ~8% to ~100% after solar electrification in Ghana
Reliability of electricity rose to >90% uninterrupted access
Community satisfaction jumped from <10% to >90%
Health worker retention, motivation, and night availability increased measurably
--------------------------------------------------------------

Healthcare systems cannot scale services, staffing, or equipment without energy
Electrification triggers follow-on investments in equipment and staffing
Energy access precedes (and enables) healthcare access, not the reverse
