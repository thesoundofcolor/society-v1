Notes for this influence:

Freedom of Speech -> Spirituality/God = 0.0

There is no evidence of a direct causal relationship between Freedom of Speech and spiritual or religious belief or practice. While Freedom of Religion is often legally and philosophically associated with Freedom of Speech, this reflects correlation and institutional co-protection rather than a causal pathway. Existing research does not demonstrate that increased speech freedom systematically increases or decreases religiosity, spirituality, or belief in God in a direct or measurable way. Any influence appears to be indirect, culturally mediated, or contingent on other variables, and is therefore insufficient to justify a pairwise effect.
