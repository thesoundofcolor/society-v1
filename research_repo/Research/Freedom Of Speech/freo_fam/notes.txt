Freedom of Speech -> Family Values = +0.05

This influence is weak and primarily indirect. Freedom of Speech supports family values by enabling open dialogue, moral reasoning, and the transmission of norms across generations. Environments that allow expression and discussion tend to encourage reflection on responsibility, ethics, and interpersonal obligations, which can reinforce family cohesion and value formation over time.

Most of this effect mediates through indirect pathways such as education, socialization, and cultural discourse rather than through immediate behavioral change. Empirical work suggests that freedom of expression expands exposure to moral perspectives and facilitates value negotiation within families, but does not by itself guarantee stronger family structures or outcomes (10.15503.jecs2023.1.249.268.pdf).

There is also evidence that legal and cultural frameworks protecting expression have an expressive effect on norms, shaping attitudes and moral commitments even in the absence of direct enforcement or incentives. These norm-signaling effects are measurable but modest, which supports assigning a small positive weight rather than a strong causal coefficient (1-s2.0-S016748702500011X-main.pdf).

Given the indirect nature of the mechanisms and the mixed empirical findings, this relationship is assigned a low positive value to reflect weak but plausible supportive pressure rather than a dominant influence.

change this later if we observe over-amplification via Religion, Education, or Public Trust; or if the model shows strong indirect loops (e.g., Speech -> Education -> Family Values)
