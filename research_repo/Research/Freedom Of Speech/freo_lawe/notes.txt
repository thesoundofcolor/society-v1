Freedom of Speech -> Law Enforcement = +0.12

This is a slight positive influence, operating primarily through indirect but measurable pathways. Freedom of Speech improves law enforcement by enabling whistleblowing, internal criticism, public scrutiny, and media oversight. These mechanisms increase accountability and institutional legitimacy rather than directly improving enforcement capacity.

Higher transparency and protected expression tend to improve public trust, which reduces hostility and increases voluntary compliance during police–public interactions. While unrestricted speech can introduce short-term friction, suppression of speech consistently damages legitimacy and cooperation.

Due to the indirect nature of the mechanisms and mixed contextual effects, this relationship is assigned a modest positive value rather than a strong causal weight.
