Notes for this influence:

Gun Rights -> Market Freedom = +0.07

Expanded gun rights modestly increase market freedom by reducing regulatory friction in the firearms sector and reinforcing norms around property rights and voluntary exchange. The effect is real but mostly confined to that sector and does not strongly reshape the broader economy.

There are balancing forces; higher security costs, insurance risk, or regulatory pushback, all that prevent this from becoming a large systemic driver.

Assigned a small positive value of +0.07 to reflect a consistent but limited structural influence.

**Shaping:
Mildly non-linear: Gains are strongest early. Beyond that, the effect flattens and can even invite counter-pressure that eats into the benefits.
