Notes for this influence:

Gun Rights -> Public Trust = -0.05

Expanded gun rights have a mild downward pull on overall public trust. While they increase perceived autonomy for some groups, widespread firearm normalization can signal institutional weakness (“the state can’t protect us”) and intensify polarization, both of which erode shared civic trust.

The effect is not large because trust shifts are asymmetric, some citizens feel more respected and empowered, others feel less secure. Net impact remains slightly negative at the societal level.

**Higher Order Pairing:
Highly conditional and identity-driven. Effects diverge by partisan alignment and baseline institutional trust. Higher-order influences (crime rates, law enforcement quality, media intensity, political polarization) will amplify or dampen this pathway significantly.
