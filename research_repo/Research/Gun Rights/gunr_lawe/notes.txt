Gun Rights -> Law Enforcement = -0.17

Expanded gun rights (particularly permitless or shall-issue carry) correlate with modest increases in institutional strain on law enforcement in dense environments. Observed mechanisms include:

Increased officer exposure to armed encounters,,
Increased gun theft and illegal circulation,
Modest decline in violent crime clearance rates,
Increased violent crime in large urban centers,

These effects are not catastrophic but are directionally negative.

*******Measurement Blind Spot******
Recorded crime data captures incidents that crossed a threshold (reportable, prosecutable, violent). It does not capture:

Crimes deterred before initiation,
Aborted crimes due to perceived firearm risk,
Defensive gun use without injury or report,
Situations where armed civilians indirectly improved officer safety,

Deterrence effects are structurally undercounted because non-events are not observable in administrative datasets. This means that the empirical literature likely overrepresents escalation and circulation effects relative to deterrence effects.

It is my controversial opinion, one that is impossible to prove, that guns may prevent more crimes than are used to commit them.

The -0.17 assignment for this influence reflects acknowledgment of this asymmetry while anchoring to observable net institutional outcomes and available research data.

***Possible Structural Shapings:

This interaction is nonlinear.
