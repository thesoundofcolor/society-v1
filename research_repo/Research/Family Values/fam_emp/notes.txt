Family Values -> Employment = +0.02

Family values exert a small, primarily indirect positive influence on employment. Healthy family environments can foster structure, self-discipline, and social skills that marginally increase employability at the individual level. However, these effects are weak relative to institutional, economic, and labor-market factors, and in some contexts family-oriented norms may offset participation. As a result, the net influence on overall employment rates is minimal but slightly positive.
