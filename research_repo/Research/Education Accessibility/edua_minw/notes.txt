Notes for this influence:

"Studies show that increases in the
average years of education or the proportion of college graduates correlate with decreases in the real minimum
wage across US states, with estimates around a 0.4% to 1.6% reduction per percentage point increase in education"
(Pargianas, 2016; Pargianas, 2018; Liu, 2025)

 This relationship is explained by voter preferences and labor market
dynamics, where more educated populations favor lower minimum wages, possibly due to reduced reliance on
minimum wage jobs. The effect is direct rather than primarily indirect, as education shapes labor market
composition and wage-setting preferences.
