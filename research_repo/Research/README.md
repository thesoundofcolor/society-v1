Methodology & Scope Disclaimer

This model is a conceptual influence network, not a predictive or empirical measurement system. The weights used in the influence matrix are normalized heuristics intended to represent the direction and relative strength of relationships between social, economic, and psychological variables, as informed by academic research, historical analysis, and common-sense systems reasoning.

No claim is made that individual weights correspond to measurable real-world coefficients, nor that any single variable acts in isolation. Many modeled relationships are probabilistic, indirect, context-dependent, or bidirectional, and their numerical representation reflects relative influence under typical conditions, rather than deterministic causation.

Where empirical literature is strong and consistent, influence magnitudes are correspondingly higher. Where evidence is mixed, indirect, or limited to analogous domains (e.g., micro-level studies, longitudinal trends, or cross-cultural comparisons), relationships are expressed as weak to moderate influences or omitted entirely.

Zeros within the matrix do not imply the absence of any real-world relationship, but rather indicate insufficient evidence, excessive contextual dependence, or redundancy via intermediate variables, and are an intentional modeling choice to avoid overfitting or narrative bias.

The model is designed for exploration, comparison, and qualitative reasoning about complex systems. It is not intended to prescribe policy, forecast specific outcomes, or resolve normative disputes. Interpretation of model outputs should always consider assumptions, boundary conditions, and the limitations inherent to simplified representations of socio-political systems.
