Equality -> Military = +0.04

Increased equality within military organizations, most often pursued through gender inclusion policies, can slightly improve cohesion, morale, and perceptions of organizational integrity. Inclusive environments may reduce internal friction and improve trust at the unit level. However, these effects are limited and often counterbalanced by resistance, cultural friction, and concerns about efficiency or standards. The evidence does not show a clear or consistent improvement in overall military effectiveness. As a result, equality exerts only a small net positive influence on the military, primarily through cohesion rather than combat capability
