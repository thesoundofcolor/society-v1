Equality → Poverty = -0.24

equality exerts a negative influence on poverty, meaning it directly reduces poverty. This is driven primarily by income equality: empirical evidence shows that reductions in Gini index stats are associated with lower rates of extreme poverty at both national and global scales.

Other forms of equality, such as racial, ethnic, gender, and LGBTQ+ equalities have a less direct relationship with poverty reduction. While they do not independently drive large changes in poverty metrics, they contribute indirectly by improving access, reducing exclusion, and reinforcing the broader conditions under which poverty reduction occurs.
