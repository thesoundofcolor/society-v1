Addiction → Spirituality/God = -0.17 [01-11-26 ~Evan L.]

Active addiction exerts a negative influence on spirituality and belief in a higher power. While individuals experiencing addiction may retain nominal or cultural belief, the literature consistently shows that addiction is associated with weakened, conflicted, or instrumental forms of spirituality. Loss of self-regulation, identity fragmentation, guilt, and alienation—core features of addiction, undermine the psychological conditions that support stable and meaningful transcendent belief.

Evidence indicates that increases in spirituality typically occur after individuals enter recovery or pursue abstinence, often through structured intervention or personal commitment. These gains should therefore be attributed to recovery processes rather than addiction itself. As a result, the net influence of addiction on spirituality is modeled as negative, with positive spiritual development treated as a conditional rebound that follows reduction in addictive behavior rather than arising from it.
