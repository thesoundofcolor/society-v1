
Addiction → Crime = +0.30

Substance use disorders show a strong and consistent association with criminal activity. Crimes may arise from intoxication effects, impaired judgment, or economic pressure to finance substance use. Criminal justice populations consistently show high prevalence of addiction-related disorders, and multiple crime categories are elevated among individuals with substance abuse problems. 

see "sar-9-001.pdf" in repo

Given the strength and directness of this relationship in the literature, a moderately strong positive influence is reasonable.

- Linear for this could totally work.

  - low addiction → minimal crime influence
    mid addiction → sharp increase
    high addiction → strong influence
