Notes for Addiction -> Cost of Living influence:

Addiction -> Cost of Living = +0.15

This is a moderate positive direct influence.  Most of the actual influence is indirect.

The connection here is not really about prices directly, but about the economic burden addiction places on society as a whole. Health care utilization, lost productivity, disability, crime-related costs, housing instability, and other downstream pressures all contribute to economic strain that ultimately exerts a positive affordability pressure. (negative effect (a bad thing), but positive weight; more cost of living)

This is a distributed effect rather than a direct pricing mechanism, so the weight shouldn't be too high. Still, the cumulative burden across multiple sectors seems large enough that a moderate positive influence is justified.

This is likely nonlinear in reality. Small levels of addiction are manageable, but once prevalence rises the economic and social burden compounds more quickly.
