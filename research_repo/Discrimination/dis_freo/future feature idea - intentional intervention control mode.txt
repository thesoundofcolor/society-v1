Future Modeling Note — Intentional Intervention Mode
In the current system, direct user manipulation of a variable represents a change in the 
societal state itself. For example, lowering Discrimination simply indicates that 
discriminatory behaviors and conditions are less prevalent within the modeled society. The 
model does not currently distinguish how such reductions occur.

However, in real societies, equivalent state outcomes may arise through very different causal 
mechanisms. Discrimination may decline organically through cultural change, economic integration, 
or educational progress, or it may decline through deliberate interventions such as legislation, 
enforcement mechanisms, or institutional pressure.

These different pathways can produce distinct secondary effects. For example, reductions in 
discrimination achieved through social or legal intervention may impose additional constraints 
on other societal variables,such as Free Speech, through mechanisms like harassment law, 
speech regulation, or institutional enforcement. By contrast, reductions produced organically 
through upstream social changes may have little or no direct impact on expressive freedom.

A future extension of the system may introduce an Intentional Intervention Mode at the 
control layer. In this mode, user adjustments would represent deliberate societal actions rather 
than passive state changes. This distinction would allow the model to explore differences between 
organic social evolution and policy-driven intervention, potentially increasing realism in scenarios 
where causal pathways meaningfully affect downstream outcomes.

This feature is deferred in the current version to preserve interpretability and avoid conflating 
state variables with intervention mechanics during early model development.
