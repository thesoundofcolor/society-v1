Notes for this influence:

Freedom of Speech -> Liberty = +0.4 [maximum magnitude]

Freedom of Speech is defensibly the most direct and foundational pillar of Liberty. Unlike many social variables that operate primarily through mediation, this relationship is immediate and structural. Liberty, as a lived condition, cannot exist without the ability to express beliefs, dissent, preferences, and identity without coercion or punishment.

Across liberal political theory, constitutional law, and historical analysis, Freedom of Speech is repeatedly identified as a constitutive element of liberty rather than a secondary support. Where speech is constrained, liberty collapses rapidly, even when other formal rights remain intact. Conversely, where speech is robustly protected, individual liberty persists even under institutional strain.

This influence is not merely normative. Empirical and historical cases consistently show that restrictions on speech precede or accompany the erosion of civil liberties, democratic participation, and personal autonomy. Free speech enables self-governance, resistance to tyranny, minority protection, and the correction of power asymmetries. It is both a shield against domination and a prerequisite for meaningful freedom.

For these reasons, and in line with the literature, this value is intentionally set at the maximum** allowed magnitude. Any lower assignment would understate the structural dependency of Liberty on Freedom of Speech. 

**This edge is capped only to prevent cascade instability or over-amplification within the Society V1 system, not due to uncertainty about its strength.
