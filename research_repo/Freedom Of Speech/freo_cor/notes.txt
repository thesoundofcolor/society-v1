This is a strong, direct negative influence. Freedom of Speech reduces corruption by enabling public scrutiny, investigative journalism, and independent media oversight. Open speech and a free press increase the probability that corrupt behavior is exposed, raising reputational, legal, and political costs for both public officials and private actors, which deters corrupt practices.

Empirical research consistently shows a causal relationship between higher levels of press and speech freedom and lower levels of corruption across regions and institutional contexts. Cross-country and firm-level studies demonstrate that greater media freedom is associated with fewer bribery incidents, reduced collusive corruption, and lower reported corruption in interactions with government officials—particularly in areas such as taxation and construction permitting *(1-s2.0-S0939362521000704-main.pdf)

The effectiveness of Freedom of Speech is amplified when paired with strong democratic institutions and credible law enforcement. In such environments, transparency generated by free expression translates into real accountability, making Freedom of Speech one of the most impactful structural constraints on corruption identified in the literature.

Supported by a broad body of work, including Brunetti & Weder (2003), Chowdhury (2004), Bhattacharyya & Hodler (2015), and subsequent analyses, the evidence indicates that restrictions on speech and media coverage correlate with higher corruption, while greater speech protections act as a powerful suppressive force.
