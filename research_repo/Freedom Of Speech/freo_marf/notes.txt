Freedom of Speech -> Market Freedom = +0.18

Freedom of Speech influences Market Freedom primarily through indirect pathways, but there is also a measurable direct effect. Markets rely on information to function efficiently. Free speech enables the dissemination of price signals, risk assessments, criticism, advertising, innovation claims, and warnings about fraud or abuse. When speech is constrained, markets become less transparent, less competitive, and more prone to inefficiency or capture.

The strongest mechanism is informational flow. Investors, consumers, and businesses make better decisions when information can move freely and be contested openly. This improves capital allocation, product quality, and long-term market resilience. Restrictions on speech, whether through censorship, licensing, or suppression of commercial expression, introduce distortions similar to price controls or information asymmetries.

That said, this relationship is not absolute or purely linear. Free speech alone does not guarantee free markets, nor does it replace institutional factors such as property rights, contract enforcement, or competition policy. In some contexts, unmoderated information environments can also introduce noise, signaling failures, or strategic manipulation. For these reasons, the influence is meaningful but bounded rather than maximal.

Accordingly, this edge is assigned a moderate positive value reflecting speech’s role as an enabling condition for market freedom rather than a sole determinant.
