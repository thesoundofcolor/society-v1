Freedom of Speech -> Discrimination = +0.08

This influence is weak and largely indirect. Current research does not demonstrate a clear or robust causal pathway from Freedom of Speech to discriminatory behavior. Much of the existing literature treats “hate speech” as a loosely defined category and often assumes downstream harms without employing methodologies capable of isolating causation from correlation.

While Freedom of Speech protects expression that may include prejudicial or offensive content, this alone does not produce discrimination. At most, higher speech freedom may marginally reduce institutional friction against the expression of discriminatory attitudes, allowing other social, cultural, or political mechanisms to operate with less resistance. This does not imply that speech freedom itself generates discriminatory outcomes, only that it may lower barriers to articulation; It allows discrimination to proliferate more unimpeded.

Conversely, low Freedom of Speech functions as a strong frictional force by penalizing certain forms of expression, creating deterrence effects at the speech level. However, suppression of expression does not demonstrate elimination of underlying discriminatory beliefs, only their reduced visibility.

Given the lack of rigorous causal evidence and the heavy reliance on normative assumptions within the literature, this relationship is assigned a small positive weight. The value reflects weak permissive pressure rather than a direct or structural influence. This is my rational assumption based on vigorous logical framing. It can be lowered or even increased during tuning.
