Notes for this influence:

Freedom of Speech -> Military = 0.0

There is no evidence of a direct causal relationship between Freedom of Speech and military effectiveness or capacity. While service members retain limited speech rights, military institutions explicitly constrain expression to preserve discipline, hierarchy, and operational readiness. Existing research treats speech primarily as a variable managed by the military rather than a driver of military performance. Any influence occurs indirectly. There is insufficient evidence or research to justify a pairwise effect.
