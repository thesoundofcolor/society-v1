Notes for this influence:

Equality -> Education Quality = +0.2 [01-08-26 ~Evan L.]

This influence reflects a modest but consistent relationship rather than a direct causal driver. The literature supports the idea that greater equality, particularly socioeconomic and gender equity, improves the effectiveness and fairness of education systems, primarily by reducing performance gaps and raising baseline outcomes rather than dramatically increasing top-end achievement. Much of the evidence is correlational and system-level, relying on multilevel modeling and perceptual or contextual indicators rather than controlled causal proof. For that reason, the weight is intentionally conservative: equality acts as a conditioning factor that improves how education functions overall, not as a primary lever of instructional quality itself.
