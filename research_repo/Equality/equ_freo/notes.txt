Equality -> Freedom of Speech = +0.7  [01-08-26 ~Evan L.]

This is a meaningful but nuanced influence. Equality and freedom of speech often reinforce one another, particularly in protecting the ability of minority voices to be heard without fear of exclusion or retaliation. In more equal systems, individuals are more likely to feel safe expressing dissenting or unpopular views.

The relationship is not simple, however. Efforts to enforce equality can sometimes require limiting forms of speech that discriminate against or dehumanize others, which creates an apparent tension at the individual level. Based on the literature, I interpret this not as a reduction of free speech overall, but as a shift in who meaningfully has access to it. Increasing equality tends to elevate individuals’ social standing and agency, which in turn expands practical, lived freedom of expression across the population.

If Authoritarianism is high and equity is increased, we may negatively influence Freedoms.
